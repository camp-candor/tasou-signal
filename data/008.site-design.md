### D. Style Guide

This guide establishes the consistent visual language for the Astro site.

#### **Typography**

*   **Primary Headings (`h1`, `h2`):** `Crimson Pro` or `Playfair Display`. A classic, high-contrast serif font. Evokes old print, elegance, and history.
*   **Body Text (`p`):** `Lato`, `Source Sans Pro`, or `Roboto`. A clean, highly readable sans-serif to contrast with the ornate headings and ensure accessibility.
*   **Accent / Thematic Font:** `UnifrakturMaguntia` or a similar Blackletter/Gothic script font. To be used *very sparingly* for single, impactful words or the campaign title logo. Evokes old manuscripts and a sense of dread.
*   **Monospace (`code`):** `Roboto Mono` for any in-character "technical" details or coordinates in session logs.

#### **Color Palette**

The palette is desaturated and atmospheric, with a single, stark accent for contrast.

*   **`Bone White` (Primary Background):** `#FDF6E3` - The color of aged vellum and sun-bleached bone.
*   **`Charcoal Black` (Primary Text):** `#212121` - A soft, deep black, not a harsh `#000`. The color of soot and shadow.
*   **`Moss Green` (Secondary Accent):** `#556B2F` - A dark, olive green. Used for links, subtle UI elements, and foliage motifs. Evokes decay and the swamp.
*   **`Blood Red` (Primary Accent / DANGER):** `#880808` - A deep, dark red. Used *only* for critical calls to action, warnings, or the "active" state of a link. Represents the genre's visceral horror.
*   **`Sepia` (Image Overlay):** `rgba(112, 66, 20, 0.4)` - Used as a transparent overlay on images to give them a faded, antique look.

#### **Imagery & Textures**

*   **Photography:** High-contrast black and white or heavily desaturated. Subject matter should focus on ruined architecture, tangled nature (Spanish moss, cypress knees), and desolate landscapes. Heavy use of **vignetting** is encouraged.
*   **Backgrounds:** Subtle, tileable textures of parchment, cracked plaster, linen, or wood grain should be used to break up solid colors and prevent the design from feeling too digital or clean.

#### **UI Elements & Interaction**

*   **Buttons:** Rectangular with sharp corners. Solid `Charcoal Black` with `Bone White` text. On hover, the background color should not change, but a 1px `Blood Red` border should fade in.
*   **Links:** `Moss Green` by default. On hover, they gain a `Blood Red` underline.
*   **Cards:** Slight `box-shadow` to create depth. No rounded corners. The `border` can be styled to look like a slightly torn or deckled edge.
*   **Transitions:** All transitions should be slow `fade` effects (e.g., `transition: opacity 0.5s ease-in-out;`). No bouncing, sliding, or popping animations. The goal is a ghostly, deliberate pace.

### Concept 2: The Cartographer's Astrolabe (A Clockwork of Damnation)

**Metaphor:** The website is not a document, but a strange, occult, clockwork device. It's a brass-and-ivory astrolabe or orrery that maps the metaphysical landscape of the campaign, not just the physical one. The user is an acolyte trying to interpret its movements.

    *   **Core Textures:** Cracked stone, polished marble, dark wood grain, aged vellum.

*   **Interaction:**
    *   The ring's slow rotation brings each spirit into view.
    *   **Hover:** The spirit turns to face the user. Its `opacity` increases, and its features become momentarily distinct. A single, mournful church bell toll is heard. The character's name appears beneath it in a ghostly script.
    *   The visual state of the sprite directly reflects their status: `Wandering` sprites are solid and upright; `Sheltered` sprites are semi-transparent and seated; `Given to the Swamp` sprites are distorted, water-logged, and trailing SVG mist tendrils.

#### 6. `<CentralBasin_ScryingMap>` (The World Map)
*   **Layout:** A stationary, circular `div` at the absolute center.
*   **Layers (Bottom to Top):**
    1.  **Obsidian Base:** A `div` with a background image of polished, cracked obsidian. The map lines are an intricate SVG overlay.
    2.  **Water Surface:** A `<canvas>` element running a simple WebGL shader to create realistic, dark water ripples that react to the cursor.
    3.  **Landmark Projections:** When a location is discovered, a CSS-animated element is added, creating a pulsing green light effect emanating from the location's "crack" on the obsidian layer below.
*   **Interaction:**
    *   **Scry (Click-and-Hold):** The water shader animation intensifies, the ripples clear out from the cursor, and the `opacity` of the water layer decreases, revealing the map etched on the obsidian below. Releasing the click causes the murk to return.
    *   **Landmark Focus:** Clicking on a light projection causes a **Divination Card** (an ornate, styled `div`) to fade in on the water's surface, displaying the landmark's info.

#### 7. `<PactAuthenticator>` (Sign In)
*   **Layout:** A small, circular `div` containing a keyhole icon, positioned at the dead center of the Scrying Basin, but with `opacity: 0` and `pointer-events: none` initially.
*   **Function:** A global JS listener tracks user interactions (e.g., `ringSpins > 2`, `scryAttempts > 3`). Once a threshold is met, this component's `opacity` is animated to `1`, and the audio cue ("The Orrery thirsts...") is played. Clicking it triggers the full-screen Clerk modal.

### C. Style Guide: The Aesthetic of Beautiful Decay

*   **Typography:**
    *   **Grand Titles/Headings:** `Cinzel Decorative`. Evokes chiseled stone and ancient grandeur.
    *   **Body Text/Logs:** `EB Garamond` (Italic). For a classic, literary, and slightly mournful feel.
    *   **Cryptic Labels/Rumors:** `UnifrakturMaguntia`. A blackletter font for that authentic, occult manuscript look.

*   **Color Palette:**
    *   **`--color-swamp-night` (Background):** `#0a0f0d` - A near-black, saturated with the darkest of greens.
    *   **`--color-marshlight` (Primary Glow):** `#6B8E23` - A sickly, phosphorescent olive green. Used for glows, highlights, the cursor wisp, and landmark projections.
    *   **`--color-petrified-wood` (Structure):** `#3D2B1F` - A deep, dark, desaturated brown for the wooden rings.
    *   **`--color-rusted-iron` (Structure):** `#4a2a2a` - A dark rust color for the metal rings.
    *   **`--color-memory-gold` (Projections/Highlights):** `#C9B474` - A warm, non-metallic gold for the Memory Lantern projections and important text.
    *   **`--color-ghost-white` (Spectral Text):** `#a9a9a9` - A low-contrast, ethereal gray for spirit names and secondary text.

*   **Textures & Materials:**
    *   All elements must have a texture. Use high-resolution seamless textures for **petrified wood, pitted rust, polished obsidian, cracked bone/ivory, and murky water caustics**.
    *   **Light:** Light should always feel motivated. It comes *from* the lanterns, the marshlight, the stars. Use CSS `box-shadow` and `radial-gradient` extensively to create glows and falloffs.

*   **Animation & Interaction:**
    *   **Pacing:** Majestic and slow. Rotations are measured in minutes, not seconds. Fades are long and gentle.
    *   **Sound:** Every major interaction has a sound. Dragging the outer ring `screeches`. Hovering a spirit `tolls a bell`. Scrying into the basin creates a `wet, sucking sound`. The audio is 50% of the experience.
    *   **The Uncanny Glitch:** The "Intrusion of the Real" is a key event. It will be a JS function that applies a temporary class to the main `<body>`. This class triggers a short, violent CSS animation involving `filter: invert(1) saturate(10);`, `transform: skew(-10deg);`, and a chromatic aberration effect simulated with `text-shadow`. It should be jarring and brief.


The final design synthesizes the high-performance, reusable structure of the earlier blueprints with the dominant, high-impact thematic aesthetic of the more detailed Southern Gothic concepts. The result is a page that achieves visual grandeur without relying on excessive client-side JavaScript.

## The Blackwood Parish Almanac: A Digital Artifact of Southern Gothic Horror

A new website design, "The Blackwood Parish Almanac," offers a chillingly immersive experience steeped in the aesthetic of Southern Gothic horror. The homepage is conceived not as a modern website, but as a decaying, interactive artifact—a found parish ledger from a forgotten swampland town. It combines narrative-driven design with unsettling interactive elements to draw users into its haunted world.

The visual foundation of the site is a high-resolution scan of stained, yellow-brown paper, with a subtle, looping video that simulates the unsteady flicker of a gas lamp. This creates a sense of unease, as shadows on the page appear to subtly writhe and shift. The color palette is dominated by deep, earthy tones like woody brown, charcoal, and dusty grays, accented with the stark, alarming color of dried blood for key interactive elements. Typography reinforces the theme, using a bleeding, spidery ink script for titles and a mix of formal, distressed serif fonts for body text, evoking a sense of historical decay and frantic, handwritten accounts.

### An Unfolding Narrative Map

The centerpiece of the homepage is "The Survey," an interactive map designed to look like a developing daguerreotype of a vast, untamed swampland. Instead of a digital overlay, a milky, chemical-like "fog of war" obscures unexplored territories. As the user moves their cursor, this fog swirls, offering maddening, half-formed glimpses of what lies within—a skeletal tree, the roof of a sunken house—before vanishing. Discovered locations are rendered with disturbing sharpness. Clicking on a landmark, such as a crumbling plantation manor or a gnarled hanging tree, causes the image to ripple and reveals a tattered, tarot-like card with the location's name, its grim status (e.g., "Forsaken"), and a link to "Bear Witness."

### A Chronicle of Misfortune

Below the map, the site flows into "The Testaments," a collection of session logs presented as torn journal pages. Each entry features a different handwriting style, some elegant and spidery, others a barely legible scrawl, complete with whiskey stains and smudges of dried mud. These accounts detail past expeditions with a title, a "Reckoning" of souls lost, and a harrowing summary of events. The link to read the full account is not a button but a smudged, dark ink thumbprint.

The site also features "The Census," a grim roster of all characters, with portraits styled like Victorian post-mortem photographs. Each entry lists the character's status as `Active`, `Retired`, or chillingly, `Given to the Swamp`.

### An Atmosphere of Dread

The design intentionally employs unconventional navigation to enhance the sense of mystery and unease. The main menu consists of labels that look scorched into the aged paper: "The Survey," "The Testaments," "The Census," and "Whispers & Warnings." The typical "Sign In" is replaced with a wrought-iron keyhole icon bearing the inscription, "Inscribe Your Name."

The experience is built on principles of atmospheric and narrative-driven design, where the interface itself tells a story. By concealing information and forcing users to uncover the world through exploration, the design aims to create a memorable and deeply unsettling journey into the heart of a fallen world. The final call to action reinforces this, eschewing a typical invitation for a somber challenge: **"The Almanac has empty pages. The Census has names yet to be entered. Will you offer your testimony?"** Clicking this fades the screen to black before presenting a stark, formal sign-in page, completing the illusion of signing one's name into a haunted ledger.
